About Me

My name is Mario DeLaPaz. I'm an NJIT Grad with a Computer Science Degree.

Having studied at NJIT, I was fortunate enough to join the Society of Hispanic Profressional Engineers (SHPE). I started as a Freshman Representative and worked my way up to Vice President by Junior year. I was also involved with the soccer club on campus and became President of the club by Sophmore year.

After undergrad, I joined the SHPE NJ Profressional Chapter eboard for about a year and then transitioned into a member. While I was working at Verizon, I joined their Hispanic ERG called Hispanic Support Organization (HSO). I also became active within the HISPA community and volunteered giving talks to middle school students promoting higher education and STEM related careers. By my last year I was given the most active member award.

SHPE helped shape my future both profressionally and personally. Being in SHPE helped land my first internship with Verizon and also where I found my extended family.

I'm now involved in event planning with the local NJIT Alumni Association chapter.

Outside of work, I enjoy playing and watching soccer. I also try to keep up with tech and tools/best practices to living a productive lifestyle. I've been trying to read more often this year so I've created a Reading List. If I'm not trying to plan my day, I'm out doing it.
